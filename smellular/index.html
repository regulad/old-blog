Why are bees dying?

You've seen it in the news, and it's sad. Bees are dying at an alarming rate. The main reason is a phenomenon called Colonony collapse disorder, or CCD. It was first obserbed in early 2006. (The year I was born!)

In 1990, the number of honey bee colonies was in the 2-3 millions. Now, its in the 1-2 millions.

What can you do?

Although many things reguarding the bee-pocalypse are out of our human control, you can do many things to support the research and protection of dying bees.

  • Buy products grown without pesticides. Colonies can die when the food that the workers feed to the larvae has pesticides.
  • Bees don't want to eat you. They want to eat the nectar from flower. Give them something to eat!
